Mutual Fund Categories

Mutual Funds • Topic 4

Just like ordering food, you need to know the menu. SEBI has defined strict categories so you know exactly where your money is going.

1. Equity Funds (High Risk, High Return)

Large Cap

Invests in Top 100 companies (Reliance, HDFC). Stable, steady growth (10-12%). Least risky in equity.

Mid Cap

Invests in 101st-250th companies. Higher growth potential (12-15%) but volatile.

Small Cap

Invests in 251st onwards. Massive growth potential (15%+) but can crash 50% in a bear market.

Flexi Cap (Recommended)

The Fund Manager has freedom. They can buy Large, Mid, or Small cap stocks based on market conditions. Best for beginners.

2. ELSS (Tax Saving Funds)

Equity Linked Savings Scheme

This is the only MF that gives you a tax deduction under Section 80C (up to ₹1.5 Lakh).

  • Lock-in: 3 Years (Lowest among all tax-saving options like PPF/FD).
  • Returns: Market linked (High potential).

3. Debt & Hybrid Funds

  • Debt Funds: Lend money to Govt/Companies. Returns (6-8%) are better than FD but lower than Equity. Safer.
  • Hybrid Funds: Mix of Equity + Debt. Good for conservative investors who want some growth with safety.

Which one for YOU?

  • Goal: Tax Saving? → Go for ELSS.
  • Goal: Long Term (>5 years)? → Go for Flexi Cap or Index Fund.
  • Goal: Short Term (<3 years)? → Avoid Equity. STICK TO DEBT/FD.

    • Ultra-Short Summary

    • Flexi Cap is the "One Size Fits All" solution for most investors.
    • Use ELSS if you need to save tax.
    • Don't put short-term money in Small/Mid cap funds.
