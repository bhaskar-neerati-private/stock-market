Moving Averages (MA & EMA)

Technical Analysis • Topic 11

A Moving Average is a technical indicator that smooths out price action by filtering out the "noise" of random short-term price fluctuations. It is a trend-following indicator because it is based on past prices.

Definition

  • SMA (Simple Moving Average): The average price over a specific number of days.
  • EMA (Exponential Moving Average): Similar to SMA but gives more weight to recent prices, making it react faster.

Simple Explanation

  • The "Traffic" Filter: Imagine looking at the stock price as a zig-zag graph of every single car on a road. The Moving Average is the average speed of all those cars. It shows you the overall flow of traffic, not the movement of one single aggressive driver.
  • Trend Compass: If the price is above the MA, the trend is UP. If below, the trend is DOWN.

Real-Life Analogy

The Fitness Journey

Imagine you weigh yourself every morning. Some days you weigh more because of water retention; some days less because you missed a meal.

  • Daily Weight: Stock Price (Volatile/Noisy)
  • 7-Day Average: Moving Average (The True Trend)

If your daily weight is higher today but your 7-day average is consistently falling, you are still successfully losing weight! The average tells the real story.

The Golden Numbers

20-Day EMA

Used by short-term traders for momentum. Often called the "institutional line."

50-Day MA

The medium-term trend. Used to spot healthy corrective dips in an uptrend.

200-Day MA

The Ultimate Baseline. If the price is below this, the stock is in a "Bear Market". Pro investors rarely buy long-term if price is below this.

Core Interpretation Rules

  • Price stays above MA → Buyers are in control.
  • MA is sloping upwards → The trend is healthy.
  • Price is too far above the MA → The stock is "Overextended" and might pull back soon ("Mean Reversion").

Ultra-Short Summary

  • MA = Price Smoothing Tool.
  • EMA = Fast reaction. SMA = Slow & Stable.
  • Price > 200-Day MA = Bullish. Price < 200-Day MA=Bearish.
