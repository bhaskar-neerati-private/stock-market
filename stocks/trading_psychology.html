Trading Psychology

Mindset • Topic 20

90% of success in the stock market is Psychology; only 10% is Strategy. You can have the best analysis in the world, but if you panic, you will lose.

Why Mindset Matters

The market is designed to transfer money from the Impatient to the Patient. Controlling your emotions (EQ) is more important than your IQ.

The Two Great Enemies

Greed (FOMO)

Buying at the top because "everyone else is making money." Usually leads to buying high.

Fear (Panic)

Selling at the bottom because the news is terrifying. Usually leads to selling low.

Common Cognitive Biases

  • Confirmation Bias: Only reading news that supports your stock and ignoring negative news.
  • Loss Aversion: Holding onto a gubit stock (losing trade) hoping it comes back to "breakeven," while selling winning stocks too early.
  • Recency Bias: Thinking the market will keep going up just because it went up last week.

    • Real-Life Analogy

    The Supermarket Line

    You join a checkout line (Buy a stock).

    • Suddenly, the line next to you moves faster (Another stock goes up).
    • You switch lines (Sell and buy the other one).
    • As soon as you switch, your new line stops, and the old line starts moving.

    This is exactly how the market works. Jumping around due to impatience ensures you stay stuck.

    Ultra-Short Summary

    • Have a plan BEFORE (Stoploss/Target) you buy.
    • Never trade based on "Tips" or News noise.
    • Patience is the highest paying skill.
