Support & Resistance

Technical Analysis ‚Ä¢ Topic 13

Support and Resistance are the most basic and powerful concepts in technical analysis. They represent specific price levels where large groups of buyers or sellers tend to enter the market.

Definition

  • Support: A "Floor" where the price stops falling and bounces up. (Demand > Supply)
  • Resistance: A "Ceiling" where the price stops rising and falls back down. (Supply > Demand)

Simple Explanation

  • Market Memory: If a stock fell to ‚Çπ100 and then rose to ‚Çπ150, the next time it falls back to ‚Çπ100, everyone remembers: "Last time it bounced here! It's cheap!" This group memory creates a Support zone.
  • Zones, Not Lines: Think of them as thick elastic bands, not thin laser lines. The price can briefly dip through them before bouncing.

Real-Life Analogy

The Bouncing Ball

Imagine you are in a house and you drop a ball.

  • The Floor: Acts as Support. The ball hits it and bounces back up.
  • The Ceiling: Acts as Resistance. If you throw the ball up, it hits the ceiling and falls back down.

If you throw the ball hard enough to break the ceiling, you reach the second floor. Now, the old ceiling becomes your new floor.

How they Behave

At Support

Buyers become aggressive. This is where high-probability buy entries happen.

At Resistance

Profit booking happens. Sellers become aggressive. Not a good time to buy.

üí° Pro Insight: The Reversal (Polarity)

One of the most powerful trade setups is when Resistance becomes Support. After a stock breaks out above a major resistance level, it often comes back to "retest" that same level. If it bounces, that level is now strong Support.

Ultra-Short Summary

  • Support = Floor. Resistance = Ceiling.
  • Buy near Support. Sell (or don't buy) near Resistance.
  • Breakout + Retest = Safest Entry Signal.
