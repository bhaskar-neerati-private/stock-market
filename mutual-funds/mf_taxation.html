Mutual Fund Taxation

Mutual Funds • Topic 6

Profits are sweet, but taxes are bitter. Here is exactly how much the Government takes from your gains.

1. Equity Funds (Broadly)

Funds holding >65% in Indian Equity. (Large Cap, Mid Cap, ELSS, etc.)

STCG (Short Term)

Sold BEFORE 1 Year.

Tax = 20% on Profits

LTCG (Long Term)

Sold AFTER 1 Year.

Tax = 12.5%*

*Exemption: First ₹1.25 Lakh of profit in a financial year is Tax Free.

2. Debt Funds (New Rules)

Funds holding < 35% Equity. (Liquid, G-Sec, Corporate Bond Funds).

  • Rule: Added to your Income.
  • Tax Rate: As per your Slab Rate.
  • There is no LTCG benefit for pure Debt funds anymore (post-Apr 2023).

Pro Tip: Tax Harvesting

Every year, the first ₹1.25 Lakh of Long Term profit is tax-free.

Strategy: Even if you want to stay invested, you can sell ₹1.25L worth of profit and immediately buy back the units. This "resets" your buying price higher, saving you tax in the distant future.

Ultra-Short Summary

  • Hold Equity Funds for >1 Year to pay lower tax (12.5%).
  • First ₹1.25L profit every year is Free.
  • Debt Fund profits are taxed just like your Salary (Slab Rate).
