MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence)

Technical Analysis • Topic 15

MACD is a momentum/trend-following indicator that shows the relationship between two moving averages. It helps traders identify early trend reversals and momentum shifts before they appear on the price chart.

Definition

  • MACD Line: The difference between two EMAs (usually 12 and 26).
  • Signal Line: An average of the MACD line itself.
  • Histogram: The distance between the two lines.

Simple Explanation

  • The "Early Warning" System: While Moving Averages (MAs) are "lagging" indicators, MACD tries to solve this by providing signals a few steps earlier.
  • Momentum Strength: A growing histogram (bars getting taller) means the current trend is getting stronger. Bars getting shorter means the trend is losing steam.

Real-Life Analogy

The Speeding Car

Imagine a car accelerating to 100 km/h.

  • Price: The car's position on the road.
  • MACD: The car's accelerator (gas pedal).

Even if the car is still moving forward (Price is rising), if the driver takes their foot off the gas pedal (MACD turns down), you know the car will slow down or stop soon. MACD shows you what the driver (market momentum) is doing before the car (price) actually stops.

Key MACD Signals

Bullish Crossover

MACD line crosses ABOVE the Signal line. Momentum is turning positive.

Bearish Crossover

MACD line crosses BELOW the Signal line. Momentum is turning negative.

Zero-Line Cross

Above Zero = Bullish zone. Below Zero = Bearish zone.

Thumb Rules

  • Only buy when the MACD is above its Signal line.
  • The strongest buy signals happen when the Bullish Crossover occurs below the Zero line.
  • Never use MACD alone in a "Sideways" market—it will give many false signals.

Ultra-Short Summary

  • MACD = Trend + Momentum combined.
  • Early Warning system for reversals.
  • Best used alongside Support/Resistance and RSI.
