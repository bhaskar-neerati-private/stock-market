SIP vs Lumpsum

Mutual Funds • Topic 5

Should you invest ₹1 Lakh today (Lumpsum) or ₹10,000 for 10 months (SIP)? This is the most common dilemma.

SIP (Systematic Investment Plan)

You give a standing instruction to your bank to deduct a fixed amount (e.g., ₹5000) on a fixed date (e.g., 5th) every month.

Why SIP is Superior for Beginners

  • Discipline: You save before you spend.
  • No Timing: You don't care if the market is High or Low. You buy in both.
  • Psychology: When markets crash, you are happy because your SIP buys *more* units.

The Math: Rupee Cost Averaging

This is the secret sauce of SIPs.

High Price = Buy Fewer Units.
Low Price = Buy More Units.
Average Cost = Lower than Market Price!

Because you buy more when the market is down, your average cost of buying drops automatically. You don't need to predict anything.

When does Lumpsum work?

Warning: Lumpsum is risky. If you invest ₹1 Lakh today and the market falls 10% tomorrow, your portfolio is instantly red. Use Lumpsum ONLY when markets has crashed significantly (correction of >20%).

SIP vs Lumpsum Strategy

  • Salary/Monthly Income? → Always SIP.
  • Bonus/Windfall/Property Sale? → Don't do Lumpsum. Put it in a Liquid Fund and do an STP (Systematic Transfer Plan) into Equity over 6-12 months. This spreads your risk.

    • Ultra-Short Summary

    • Don't wait for "Right Time". Start an SIP.
    • SIP automates "Buying Low".
    • Avoid Lumpsum unless you are an expert at market timing.
