Price-to-Book (P/B) Ratio

Fundamental Analysis ‚Ä¢ Topic 5

The P/B Ratio compares the market's valuation of a company to its actual net worth (Book Value). It tells you if you are paying for actual assets or just for future expectations.

Definition

  • Compares share price to the book value per share.
  • Formula:
    • P/B Ratio = Current Share Price √∑ Book Value per Share

Simple Explanation

  • The "Safety Buffer": If P/B is 1.0, you are buying the company's assets at their exact cost in the books. If the company shuts down today, you should theoretically get your money back.
  • Asset Backing: It shows how much physical or financial "stuff" (factories, cash, loans) is backing each rupee of the share price.

Real-Life Analogy

The Second-Hand Car

Imagine a used car has a "Blue Book" value of ‚Çπ2 lakh based on its model and age. This is its Book Value.

  • If you buy it for ‚Çπ2 lakh, P/B = 1.0.
  • If you buy it for ‚Çπ4 lakh because it was owned by a celebrity, P/B = 2.0.

In stocks, you pay a "premium" (high P/B) for strong brands, good management, or high growth potential.

üí° Pro Insight: The P/B-ROE Synergy

A high P/B is okay only if the ROE (Return on Equity) is also high. This shows the company is generating massive profit from its assets.

  • High P/B + High ROE: This is a "Quality Growth" company.
  • Low P/B + High ROE: This is a "Hidden Gem" (Rare).
  • Low P/B + Low ROE: This is a "Value Trap" (Avoid).

Where P/B Matters Most

Banks & NBFCs

Essential metric. Their product is money/assets. A bank with P/B < 1.0 often indicates hidden bad loans.

Manufacturing

Asset-heavy businesses with large factories and machinery.

Avoid for Tech/FMCG

Software and brand-heavy companies (like Google or HUL) have few physical assets but huge profits. P/B will always be very high for them.

Ultra-Short Summary

  • P/B = Price vs Net Worth.
  • Critical for Financial and Asset-heavy sectors.
  • Always pair with ROE for the full picture.
