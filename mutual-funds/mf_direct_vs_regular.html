Direct vs Regular Plans

Mutual Funds • Topic 2

This is the single most important decision in Mutual Fund investing. Choosing the wrong plan can cost you 20-30% of your total wealth over 20 years.

What is the Difference?

Direct Plan

You buy directly from the AMC (Asset Management Company) or via platforms like Zerodha/Groww.

  • Commission: ZERO.
  • Returns: Higher.
Regular Plan

You buy through a Bank Manager or an Agent.

  • Commission: The agent gets ~1% of your money every single year forever.
  • Returns: Lower.

The Power of 1% (Compounding)

The 25 Lakh Loss

Imagine you invest ₹10,000/month for 25 years.

  • In Direct Plan (12% return): You end up with ₹1.89 Crores.
  • In Regular Plan (11% return): You end up with ₹1.62 Crores.

You lose ₹27 Lakhs just as "commission" to your agent. Is their advice worth ₹27 Lakhs?

How to Identify?

Check the name of the fund in your statement:

  • "HDFC Top 100 Fund - Direct - Growth" → ✅ GOOD
  • "HDFC Top 100 Fund - Regular - Growth" → ❌ BAD

Expense Ratio

This is the fee the Fund House charges to manage your money (Salaries, Research, Marketing).

  • Formula: Charged as a % of your total value daily.
  • Thumb Rule: Lower is better. For Active funds, < 1.0% is good. For Index funds, < 0.2% is good.

Ultra-Short Summary

  • Always select Direct - Growth plans.
  • Avoid "Regular" plans unless you absolutely need hand-holding.
  • Small 1% difference = Massive difference in long term.
