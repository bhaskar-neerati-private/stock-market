Market Capitalization

Fundamental Analysis • Topic 1

Market capitalization is the total market value of a company's outstanding shares of stock. It's essentially the "price tag" the stock market has put on the entire company.

Definition

  • Total market value of all outstanding shares.
  • It determines the size of the company in the eyes of the stock market.
  • Formula:
    • Market Cap = Current Share Price × Total Number of Shares

Simple Explanation

  • The "Size" Label: It tells you whether a company is a Giant (Blue Chip), a Medium-sized player, or a Small startup.
  • Buyout Price: If you wanted to buy 100% of the company today, the Market Cap is roughly what you'd have to pay.

Real-Life Analogy

The Pizza Analogy

Imagine a Pizza cut into 8 slices.

  • Each slice costs ₹100 (Share Price).
  • There are 8 slices (Total Shares).
  • The whole Pizza costs ₹800 (Market Cap).

The price of one slice doesn't tell you how big the pizza is. You need both the price and the number of slices to know the total value.

Market Cap Categories

Large-Cap

Stable, established companies (e.g., Reliance, TCS). Lower risk, steady returns.

Mid-Cap

Growth-oriented companies. Medium risk, potential for higher returns.

Small-Cap

Young companies/Startups. High risk, but potential for "multi-bagger" returns.

Ultra-Short Summary

  • Market Cap = Total Company Value.
  • Share Price alone is NOT the size.
  • Crucial for risk management and portfolio balance.
