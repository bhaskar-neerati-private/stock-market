Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio

Fundamental Analysis ‚Ä¢ Topic 3

P/E Ratio shows how much investors are willing to pay for ‚Çπ1 of a company's profit. It's the most common tool used to judge if a stock is "Cheap" or "Expensive".

Definition

  • Compares the current share price to the earnings per share (EPS).
  • Formula:
    • P/E Ratio = Current Share Price √∑ Earnings Per Share (EPS)

Simple Explanation

  • Payback Years: If a company has a P/E of 20, it means the market is paying 20 times its current profit. This effectively means it would take 20 years to get your money back if profits stayed the same.
  • Expectation Gauge: High P/E means people expect big growth. Low P/E means people are cautious or see no growth.

  • Formula: P/E Ratio √∑ EPS Growth Rate
  • Interpretation:

    • PEG > 1.0: Overvalued (unless the quality is exceptionally high).

Decision Checklist

  • Is the P/E lower or equal to the Industry P/E?
  • Is the EPS growth rate higher than the P/E ratio? (PEG check)
  • Is the company's historical P/E median usually higher than current?

Ultra-Short Summary

  • P/E = Price tag of profit.
  • Always compare with Peers and Industry average.
  • Combine with PEG to see if growth justifies the price.
