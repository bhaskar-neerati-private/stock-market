Master Investment Checklist

The complete 5-step framework to validate a stock before hitting "Buy".

Step 1 Structural Integrity

Market Cap & Liquidity

Is the company large enough for your risk appetite? Ensure there is enough delivery volume so you aren't stuck in a "lower circuit" trap.

Debt-to-Equity (D/E) Check

Ideally D/E < 1.0. High debt kills companies during recessions. Check if the Interest Coverage Ratio is> 3 (can they pay their interest comfortably?).

Promoter Holding

Do the founders believe in the company? Look for > 50% holding. CRITICAL: Check if promoters have pledged (loaned against) their shares. Pledging > 25% is a major red flag.

Step 2 Valuation Reality Check

Relative P/E Ratio

Compare stock P/E with Industry P/E. A stock at 50 P/E in an industry of 20 P/E is expensive unless it's growing at 3x the industry rate.

P/B vs Book Value

For banks and manufacturing, is the Price-to-Book ratio reasonable? A P/B < 1.0 might be a bargain or a "value trap."

PEG Ratio (Pro Addition)

Price/Earnings to Growth. A PEG < 1.0 means you are paying a fair price for the growth you are getting.

Step 3 Business Efficiency

ROE & ROCE

Return on Equity (ROE) should ideally be > 15-20%. It shows how well management uses your money. ROCE shows how well they use *all* capital including debt.

Profit Margin Trends

Are Operating Profit Margins (OPM) stable or rising? Falling margins mean the company is losing its competitive edge (Moat).

Pro Tip: High ROE with High Debt is fake. Always look for High ROE with Low Debt.

Step 4 Technical Confirmation

Moving Average Alignment

Is the price above the 200-day MA? Never fight the long-term trend. Ideally, 50-day MA should be above 200-day MA (Golden Cross).

RSI & Momentum

Is RSI between 40 and 60? Avoid buying at RSI > 70 (Overbought). Catching a bounce at RSI 30 near Support is a high-probability trade.

Volume Confirmation

Was the recent price rise supported by high "Delivery Volume %"? If not, the move might be a "fake-out" by operators.

Step 5 The "Red Flag" Scan

Cash Flow Analysis

Is "Net Profit" translating into "Cash from Operations"? If a company shows profit but the bank account is empty, it's accounting manipulation.

Legal & Regulatory

Any pending SEBI investigations or major court cases against promoters? One bad legal news can wipe 50% of the value in a day.

The Final Verdict

Before you invest, ask: "If the market closes for 5 years, would I still be happy owning this?"

  • If Fundamentals are good but Technicals are bad → Wait/Watchlist
  • If Technicals are good but Fundamentals are bad → Short-term Trade Only
  • If BOTH are good → Strong Investment Candidate
