Portfolio Construction

Risk Management • Topic 19

Picking a good stock is only half the battle. Building a "Portfolio" means combining different stocks in a way that minimizes risk while maximizing returns. It prevents one bad decision from wiping out your wealth.

The Art of Diversification

  • Diversification: Spreading your money across different sectors and companies.
  • The Goal: To ensure that if one sector crashes (e.g., IT), another sector (e.g., Banking) balances it out.

Golden Rules of Allocation

  • The 5% Rule: Never put more than 5-10% of your total capital into a single stock. If it goes to zero, you only lose 5%.
  • Number of Stocks: Ideally, hold between 15 to 20 stocks. Fewer than 10 is too risky; more than 30 is impossible to track (Diworsification).
  • Correlations: Don't buy 5 different banks and call it "diversified". If RBI increases rates, all 5 will fall together.

    • Ideal Sector Allocation Model

    Core (60%)

    Stable Large-caps (Blue Chips). Consistent compounders like HDFC, Reliance, TCS.

    Growth (25%)

    Mid-caps with high potential. Companies growing fast but have proven business models.

    Satellite (15%)

    Small-caps / High Risk. Moonshot bets that could double or go to zero.

    Real-Life Analogy

    The Balanced Diet (Thali)

    Think of your portfolio like a nutritious Indian Thali.

    • Rice/Roti (Core): The carbohydrate base. Necessary for survival. (Large Caps)
    • Dal/Curry (Growth): Protein and flavor. Adds quality to the meal. (Mid Caps)
    • Pickle/Sweet (Satellite): Small amounts for kick/excitement. Too much will make you sick! (Small Caps)

    Ultra-Short Summary

    • Don't put all eggs in one basket.
    • Max 10% in one stock. Max 25% in one sector.
    • Balance safety (Large) with growth (Mid/Small).
