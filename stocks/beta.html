Beta (Stock Volatility)

Risk Management • Topic 16

Beta is a measure of a stock's volatility (risk) in relation to the overall market (e.g., Nifty 50). It tells you how sensitive a stock is to market movements.

Definition

  • Market Beta = 1.0.
  • Beta shows how much a stock moves compared to the index.

Simple Explanation

  • The "Reaction" Scale: If Beta is 1.5, and the market goes up 1%, the stock is expected to go up 1.5%. If the market falls 1%, the stock will likely fall 1.5%.
  • Aggression Meter: High Beta = Aggressive/Risky. Low Beta = Defensive/Stable.

Real-Life Analogy

The Bus Passengers

Imagine a giant Bus (The Index) driving down a bumpy road.

  • Standard Passenger (Beta 1.0): Sway slightly when the bus turns.
  • Anxious Passenger (Beta 2.0): Jumps out of their seat at every small bump. (Highly Volatile)
  • Sleeping Passenger (Beta 0.5): Barely notices the bumps. (Low Volatility)

If you don't like roller-coasters, you should look for stocks with a Beta less than 1.0.

Beta Value Interpretation

Beta < 1.0

Defensive stocks. e.g., HUL, Nestlé. They fall less when the market crashes, but rise slower in a bull run.

Beta = 1.0

Moves exactly with the market. Typical of index-heavy blue chips.

Beta > 1.0

Aggressive stocks. e.g., Small-caps, Tech startups, Adani stocks. Potential for high gains, and high losses.

Ultra-Short Summary

  • Beta = Sensitivity to Market bumps.
  • Market Beta = 1.0.
  • Choose Low Beta for safety, High Beta for aggressive growth.
