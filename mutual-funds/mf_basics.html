Mutual Funds Basics

Mutual Funds • Topic 1

A Mutual Fund is a financial vehicle that pools money from many investors to invest in securities like stocks, bonds, and short-term debt. It is managed by professional Fund Managers.

Why Mutual Funds?

  • Professional Management: You don't need to pick stocks; an expert does it for you.
  • Diversification: With just ₹500, you can own a tiny slice of 50 different companies.
  • Liquidity: You can usually withdraw your money anytime (unlike real estate).

NAV (Net Asset Value)

Think of NAV as the "Share Price" of the Mutual Fund scheme.

NAV = (Total Assets - Total Liabilities) / Total Number of Units
Myth Buster: A Low NAV (₹10) does NOT mean the fund is "Cheap" compared to a High NAV (₹500). NAV is irrelevant for returns. Only the growth % matters.

AUM (Assets Under Management)

This is the total market value of the investments managed by the fund. It shows the fund's size.

  • Too Small (< ₹500 Cr): Risky. A few investors leaving can impact the fund.
  • Too Big (> ₹50,000 Cr): Hard to manage. It's difficult for the manager to enter/exit stocks without moving the price.

Real-Life Analogy

The Buffet vs. Cooking at Home

  • Stock Trading (Cooking): You buy ingredients (Stocks), measure them, and cook. If you mess up, the food is ruined. It takes time and skill.
  • Mutual Fund (Buffet): You pay a fixed price (Investment) to a restaurant. The Chef (Fund Manager) handles the shopping and cooking. You just eat (Returns).

Ultra-Short Summary

  • MF = Pooled money managed by experts.
  • NAV is just the unit price; don't chase low NAV.
  • Perfect for people who don't have time to research stocks.
