Book Value (BV)

Fundamental Analysis • Topic 6

Book Value is the "net worth" of a company as per its balance sheet. It’s what shareholders would theoretically receive if the company sold all its assets and paid off all its debts today.

Definition

  • The total value of a company's assets that shareholders would get if the company were liquidated.
  • Formula:
    • Book Value per Share = (Total Assets − Total Liabilities) ÷ Total Shares

Simple Explanation

  • The "Shutdown Value": If the company locks its doors tomorrow, Book Value tells you the "leftover" cash for share owners.
  • Reality Check: It represents the tangible worth of the company, ignoring brand name or future growth.

Real-Life Analogy

The Home Equity Analogy

Imagine you own a house worth ₹1 crore.

  • Market Value: ₹1 crore
  • Home Loan (Debt): ₹40 lakh
  • Your Net Worth (Book Value): ₹60 lakh

Even though the "Market" thinks your house is worth ₹1 crore, your "Book Value" (what you actually own) is ₹60 lakh. If you sell the house and pay the bank, you keep ₹60 lakh.

Why Book Value Matters

  • Downside Cushion: If the share price falls below Book Value, the stock is often considered a "Deep Value" play (common in PSUs).
  • Baseline for Growth: A healthy company should grow its Book Value over time as it retains profits.
    • Warning: Falling Book Value over multiple years is a major red flag indicating consistent losses or bad management.

    Rules of Thumb

    Rising BV

    Healthy business. Profits are being reinvested or saved correctly.

    BV > Share Price

    Market is extremely pessimistic. Could be a massive opportunity or a dying company.

    Ultra-Short Summary

    • Book Value = Assets − Liabilities.
    • It is the "Accounting Reality" of the company.
    • Growth in Book Value = Growth in real wealth.
