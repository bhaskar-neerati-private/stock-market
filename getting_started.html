Getting Started & Tools

Logistics • Topic 0

You have the knowledge. Now you need the infrastructure. This page covers the "How" and "Where" of buying your first stock.

1. Prerequisites

  • PAN Card: Mandatory for all financial transactions in India.
  • Bank Account: To transfer funds to your trading account.
  • Demat Account: The digital "wallet" where your shares are stored. linked to your PAN.

    • 2. Choosing a Broker

    Discount Brokers

    (e.g., Zerodha, Groww, Upstox)

    • Pros: Low brokerage (often ₹0 for delivery), great mobile apps, modern UI.
    • Best For: DIY Investors & Beginners.
    Full-Service Brokers

    (e.g., HDFC Sec, ICICI Direct)

    • Pros: Research tips, relationship managers, 3-in-1 accounts.
    • Cons: High brokerage fees.

    4. Market vs. Limit Orders

    Beginner Mistake: Never use "Market Order" for illiquid stocks. You might end up buying at a much higher price than you see on screen.
    • Market Order: "Buy at whatever price is available RIGHT NOW." (Fast, but risky on price).
    • Limit Order: "Buy ONLY if the price is ₹100 or lower." (Safe, but order might not get filled).

    5. Taxation Basics (India)

    Any profit you make is taxable. Keep this in mind!

    • STCG (Short Term Capital Gain): If sold before 1 year. Taxed at 20%.
    • LTCG (Long Term Capital Gain): If sold after 1 year. Taxed at 12.5% (on profits above ₹1.25 Lakh).
    • Note: Tax rates change with budgets. Always check latest rules.

    Ultra-Short Summary

    • Open a Discount Broker account (Zerodha/Groww).
    • Use Screener.in for Research.
    • Use Limit Orders to buy.
    • Be aware of the taxes on your profits.
