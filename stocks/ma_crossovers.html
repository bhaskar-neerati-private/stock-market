MA Slopes & Crossovers

Technical Analysis ‚Ä¢ Topic 12

Understanding the "Slope" (direction) of a moving average and the "Crossover" (when two MAs meet) is how investors spot major shifts in market psychology before they become obvious.

Definition

  • Slope: The angle of the MA line. Upward = healthy; Downward = dangerous.
  • Crossover: When a short-term Moving Average (e.g., 50-day) crosses over a long-term one (e.g., 200-day).

Simple Explanation

  • The "Regime Change": Think of it as a captain changing the direction of a giant ship. It doesn't happen instantly, but when it does, it's a major event for everyone on board.
  • Acceleration Check: If the 50-day MA is moving up much faster than the 200-day MA, it means momentum is rapidly increasing.

The Power of Slope

Never ignore the Slope!

Even if the price is above the 200-Day MA, if the 200-Day MA itself is sloping downward, the stock is in long-term trouble. A rising slope is a sign of institutional accumulation (big money buying).

Ultra-Short Summary

  • Rising Slope = Strength. Falling Slope = Weakness.
  • Golden Cross = Strong Buy.
  • Death Cross = Major Exit.
