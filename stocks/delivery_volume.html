Delivery Volume %

Order Flow Analysis • Topic 17

Delivery Volume % differentiates between "Intraday Trading" (gambling/speculation) and "Real Buying" (investing). It shows what percentage of shares traded today were actually moved into investors' Demat accounts.

Definition

  • Traded Quantity: Total shares bought and sold today.
  • Deliverable Quantity: Shares meant to be held for more than one day.
  • Formula:
    • Delivery % = (Deliverable Quantity ÷ Total Traded Quantity) × 100

Simple Explanation

  • Trading vs. Conviction: If a stock has massive volume but very low delivery (e.g., 5%), it means people are just flipping it back and forth for quick profits. If delivery is high (e.g., 60%), it means serious investors are buying and holding.
  • Quality of Move: A price rise with high delivery is a "Healthy Rise." A price rise with low delivery is often an "Operator Trap."

Real-Life Analogy

The Car Showroom

Imagine a luxury car showroom.

  • Traded Quantity: 100 people came to test-drive the car today.
  • Deliverable Quantity: 5 people actually paid and took the car home.
  • Delivery %: 5%

If 50 people took the car home (50% delivery), it means the car is a huge hit with real buyers. If 100 people just "test-drive" but nobody buys, there is no real demand!

Why It Matters

  • Bullish Signal: Rising Price + Rising Delivery % = Big Institutions are buying.
  • Warning Signal: Rising Price + Falling Delivery % = The move is weak and might crash soon.
  • Accumulation: If Delivery % is consistently above 40-50% for several days, someone big is silently building a position.

    • Ultra-Short Summary

    • High Delivery = High Conviction.
    • Always look for delivery above 30-40% for safe investments.
    • Helps you avoid "pump and dump" stocks.
