Earnings Per Share (EPS)

Fundamental Analysis • Topic 2

EPS is the portion of a company's profit allocated to each individual outstanding share of common stock. It serves as an indicator of a company's profitability.

Definition

  • Profit earned by a company for every single share you own.
  • High EPS generally indicates better profitability.
  • Formula:
    • EPS = (Net Profit − Preferred Dividends) ÷ Total Number of Shares

Simple Explanation

  • The "Profit Slice": If a company earns ₹1,000 and has 100 shares, each share "earned" ₹10. That's your EPS.
  • Real Owner's Share: It shows how much of the profit truly belongs to you for each share you hold.

Real-Life Analogy

The Family Business Analogy

Imagine a family bakery that makes ₹10,000 profit a month. The family has 4 members who own equal shares of the business.

  • Total Profit: ₹10,000
  • Total Shares: 4
  • "Earnings Per Member": ₹2,500

If they buy more ovens and profit goes up to ₹20,000, each member's "EPS" goes up to ₹5,000. That's growth!

Why EPS Matters

  • Primary Fuel for Share Price: In the long run, the share price usually follows the direction of the EPS.
  • Dividend Foundation: A company can only pay dividends if it has earnings (EPS).
  • Growth Benchmark: Year-on-year growth in EPS is the most important sign of a healthy company.

    • Ultra-Short Summary

    • EPS = Profit per share.
    • Rising EPS = Growing wealth for shareholders.
    • Always check if EPS growth is sustainable.
