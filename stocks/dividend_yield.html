Dividend Yield

Fundamental Analysis • Topic 9

Dividend Yield is a financial ratio that shows how much a company pays out in dividends each year relative to its stock price. It’s like the "interest rate" you earn on your investment in the form of cash.

Definition

  • The percentage of return you get just from dividends.
  • Formula:
    • Dividend Yield % = (Dividend per Share ÷ Current Share Price) × 100

Simple Explanation

  • Passive Income Meter: It tells you how much cash "rent" you will get for every ₹100 you invest in the stock.
  • FD Comparison: If an FD gives 6% and a stock gives 5% dividend yield, the stock is almost acting like an FD but with the potential for the price to go up too!

Real-Life Analogy

The Rental Property Analogy

Imagine you buy a flat for ₹1 crore and you rent it out for ₹3 lakh a year.

  • Property Price: ₹1 crore
  • Yearly Rent: ₹3 lakh
  • Rental Yield: 3%

Dividend yield works exactly the same. The Dividend is the "Rent" and the Share Price is the "Property Value".

Beware: The Dividend Yield Trap

Yield Trap Warning: Sometimes a stock price crashes by 50% because the business is dying. This makes the Dividend Yield look huge (e.g., 20%).

Investors buy it thinking it's a great deal, but soon the company stops paying dividends because it has no money, and the share price falls even more. Always check if the company is actually making profits to sustain the dividend!

Ultra-Short Summary

  • Yield = Cash return on price.
  • Great for passive income seekers.
  • A high yield can sometimes be a trap—check the fundamentals!
